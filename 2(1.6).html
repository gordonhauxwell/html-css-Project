1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them

just like in every bodies day to day life we will encounter disabled / less abled people than our selves. So we need to build certain things into our websites to make it easier for there people to use properly. This will make a more comfortable and positive experience for that user in particular.

Here are 9 ways you can make your Website Disability-Friendly
  • Use alt tags. When you hover your mouse over an image on a website, the little words that pop up are called alt tags.
  • Create subtitles and transcripts.
  • Put periods in abbreviations.
  • Describe your links.
  • Utilize colour control.
  • Get clickable.
  • Keep your copy simple.
  • Include an accessibility guide.
  • Know your audience.
